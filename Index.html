Category #1: Animals

Alaska is home to all three species of North American bears: black, brown (grizzly), and polar bears. The bald eagle population in Alaska is the largest in the U.S., with thousands living along the coast. Moose are abundant in Alaska, with over 175,000 estimated to live in the state.

Category #2: Culture

Alaska Native cultures include several distinct groups, such as the Inupiat, Yup'ik, Tlingit, and Athabascan peoples. The Iditarod Trail Sled Dog Race is a major cultural event, celebrating Alaska's sled dog heritage. Many traditional Alaska Native arts, like totem carving, beadwork, and dance, are still practiced.

Category #3: Music

Alaska Native music often includes drumming and chanting as part of storytelling and cultural ceremonies. Bluegrass and folk music festivals are popular in Alaska, reflecting its frontier culture. The Anchorage Symphony Orchestra is one of Alaska's oldest musical organizations, offering classical and contemporary music.
